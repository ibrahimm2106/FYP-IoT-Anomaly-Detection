\# Final Year Project Rubric



\## Grade bands

\- Inadequate

\- Adequate

\- Good

\- Very Good

\- Excellent



\---



\# Poster



\## Clarity and suitability of problem definition (L04, LO2, L05)

\- Inadequate: Problem is vague, or not clearly defined. Suitability to a real need is absent.

\- Adequate: Problem is identifiable and described, but may lack focus or strong real-world relevance.

\- Good: Clear and focused problem definition with a demonstrable real-world need. Solution is appropriate in scope.

\- Very Good: Problem is precisely defined, highly relevant, and evidence is provided that the project addresses a significant real-world need.

\- Excellent: Problem is compelling, highly original, and defined with exceptional clarity. The project’s impact on the wider context is self-evident and well-justified.



\## Identification of key sources (LO2)

\- Inadequate: No sources cited, or sources are irrelevant/unreliable.

\- Adequate: Few sources cited; mostly introductory or general and/or lacking in academic rigour.

\- Good: Good range of sources cited, including academic/technical papers or relevant industry reports used to justify the proposed approach.

\- Very Good: Strong range of critical sources identified, demonstrating thorough synthesis of existing ideas. Key gaps in current literature/practice are noted.

\- Excellent: Comprehensive and highly critical review of existing work/literature. All key sources are identified, synthesized, and used to directly inform and justify the novelty of the proposed solution.



\## Consideration of legal, ethical and professional issues relevant to the project idea (LO6, LO2)

\- Inadequate: No mention of legal, ethical, or professional issues.

\- Adequate: Basic acknowledgment of one or two obvious issues, such as data privacy. Lacks depth or specific relevance.

\- Good: Identifies and briefly discusses relevant legal, ethical, and professional issues specific to the project domain.

\- Very Good: Thorough identification and insightful analysis of multiple issues as they affect the proposed project. Proactive mitigation strategies are outlined.

\- Excellent: Exceptional understanding of legal, ethical, and professional issues with deep critical analysis and discussion of wider social impact.



\## Clear aims and objectives (LO2)

\- Inadequate: Aims are missing or too broad to be measurable. Objectives are not provided.

\- Adequate: Aims are stated but objectives are generic or only partially measurable. Connection to the problem is weak.

\- Good: Clear and concise overall aim. Objectives directly align with solving the defined problem and project requirements.

\- Very Good: Aims and objectives are clearly articulated, highly realistic for the scope, and serve as an excellent blueprint for the project plan including evaluation and stakeholder engagement.

\- Excellent: Aims are inspirational yet grounded. Objectives are meticulously defined, highly challenging yet achievable, and clearly structured.



\## Appropriate technological solution planned (LO2, LO3)

\- Inadequate: Proposed technology is ill-suited or a simple off-the-shelf solution.

\- Adequate: Solution is generally appropriate, but details are sparse and not fully justified.

\- Good: Appropriate and justified selection of core technologies with evidence of practical and analytical skills.

\- Very Good: Well-detailed technical architecture with strong justification and clear application of advanced or specialist skills.

\- Excellent: Highly innovative and sophisticated technical plan with exceptionally robust justification.



\## Viable project plan in place (LO1)

\- Inadequate: No viable project plan.

\- Adequate: Basic plan exists but lacks detail on dependencies and risks.

\- Good: Viable project plan with logical phasing and reasonable time estimates aligned with objectives.

\- Very Good: Detailed and robust project plan with contingency planning, realistic risks, and strong alignment to aims.

\- Excellent: Exemplary project plan showing exceptional planning and project management mastery.



\## Ethical use of AI planned (LO6, LO3)

\- Inadequate: AI is used without thought for ethical implications.

\- Adequate: AI use is mentioned, but ethical considerations are basic or generic.

\- Good: Clear plan to address ethical challenges such as code quality, fairness, and transparency. Shows AI assists rather than replaces the student’s work.

\- Very Good: Thorough and critical plan for ethical AI use, with specific technical steps to mitigate bias, maintain quality, and ensure learning outcomes are met.

\- Excellent: Demonstrates leadership in ethical AI planning and explainable AI, setting a high standard for professional AI-assisted development.



\## Good communication via the poster (LO7)

\- Inadequate: Poster is confusing, poorly organised, illegible, or contains major errors.

\- Adequate: Poster is readable, but weakly structured and inconsistent in formatting.

\- Good: Poster is clear, well-organised, and visually appealing, making the overall process easy to understand.

\- Very Good: Poster is professionally designed with excellent graphics and layout.

\- Excellent: Poster is outstanding in clarity, visual impact, and conciseness, requiring minimal effort to grasp the project’s quality and significance.

\# Mid-point Review



\## Relevant and credible literature is referenced (LO2)

\- Inadequate: Few or no sources; references are mostly generic, popular articles, or Wikipedia.

\- Adequate: Minimal number of sources cited, generally relevant but lacking in high-quality academic or technical credibility.

\- Good: Appropriate range of sources cited, including high-quality academic papers, reliable technical documentation, and credible industry reports.

\- Very Good: Strong body of literature identified and referenced, demonstrating comprehensive understanding.

\- Excellent: Exceptional identification of the core, most influential literature and technical standards.



\## Literature has wide range, is well summarised, compared and analysed (LO2, LO3)

\- Inadequate: Sources are listed but not summarised or compared.

\- Adequate: Literature is briefly summarised, but comparison is superficial and analysis is descriptive.

\- Good: Literature is well summarised with clear comparison of differing approaches and evidence of analysis.

\- Very Good: Demonstrates critical analysis of a wide range of literature, with synthesis and gap identification.

\- Excellent: Highly sophisticated analysis of diverse literature, creating a strong conceptual foundation.



\## Relevance of literature review to project solution is clear (LO2, LO3, LO6, LO7)

\- Inadequate: Literature review is disconnected from the proposed solution.

\- Adequate: General link is stated, but not explicitly used to justify design decisions.

\- Good: Literature review clearly informs technical choices and problem refinement.

\- Very Good: Literature is skillfully woven into the solution rationale and design decisions.

\- Excellent: Literature review acts as the core intellectual foundation for all major design, technical, and ethical choices.



\## Competitor analysis / related work section is detailed and relevant (LO4, LO2, LO6, LO7, LO3)

\- Inadequate: Missing or very superficial.

\- Adequate: Identifies relevant related work but lacks technical detail.

\- Good: Detailed analysis of relevant existing solutions, comparing technical merits, market success, and relevant ethical issues.

\- Very Good: Comprehensive and insightful analysis used to define the project’s unique contribution.

\- Excellent: Strategically brilliant analysis of architectures, market failures, and ethical shortcomings.



\## Refinement of aims and objectives (LO5)

\- Inadequate: Aims and objectives are unchanged despite new learning.

\- Adequate: Aims/objectives are present but not clearly refined.

\- Good: Aims and objectives are clearly refined and updated based on literature and early work.

\- Very Good: Aims and objectives are well calibrated to project scope and challenges.

\- Excellent: Refinement is masterful, precise, challenging, and demonstrates proactive self-evaluation.



\## Clear rationale for technical approach (LO3)

\- Inadequate: Technical approach is vague or based on personal preference only.

\- Adequate: Tools/frameworks are listed, but rationale is weak.

\- Good: Clear, evidence-based rationale explaining why the chosen approach fits the problem.

\- Very Good: Expertly justified technical approach with comparison of alternatives and appropriate metrics.

\- Excellent: Impeccably argued rationale showing analytical and practical mastery with foresight into future challenges.



\## Clear rationale for research methods chosen (LO3, LO6)

\- Inadequate: No mention of research methods or rationale.

\- Adequate: Basic research method mentioned but weakly justified.

\- Good: Appropriate research methods selected with clear rationale and ethical awareness.

\- Very Good: Research methods are tailored to gather high-value data for design and evaluation.

\- Excellent: Research methods are innovative, rigorous, and demonstrate deep methodological insight.



\## Evidence-based design process planned (LO2, LO3, LO4)

\- Inadequate: Design process is not mentioned or is arbitrary.

\- Adequate: Basic design process is mentioned but link to user needs or literature is weak.

\- Good: Clear design process showing how evidence informs technical and user requirements.

\- Very Good: Explicitly evidence-based design process with milestones driven by synthesis and user feedback.

\- Excellent: Exemplary design process showing highly professional translation of evidence into a quality solution.



\## Development of legal, ethical, social and professional issues (LO2, LO6)

\- Inadequate: Issues are repeated without new detail or analysis.

\- Adequate: Basic discussion is present but underdeveloped.

\- Good: Issues are developed and refined in light of technical decisions and literature.

\- Very Good: In-depth discussion proactively addressing specific legal, ethical, social, and professional challenges.

\- Excellent: Outstanding, deeply critical discussion proposing innovative mitigations.



\## Identification and mitigations of risks explained (LO6, LO5, LO3, LO1, LO4)

\- Inadequate: No meaningful risks identified.

\- Adequate: Basic risks identified but mitigations are vague.

\- Good: Relevant technical, ethical, and project risks identified with clear mitigation strategies.

\- Very Good: Comprehensive risk assessment with detailed and realistic mitigations.

\- Excellent: Exceptional foresight in risk identification with robust contingency planning.



\## Correct use of the project report template (LO7)

\- Inadequate: Major template sections are missing or formatting is very poor.

\- Adequate: Template is used, but formatting is inconsistent and some sections are incomplete.

\- Good: Project report template is correctly used and formatted clearly and professionally.

\- Very Good: Excellent adherence to the template, producing a cohesive and highly readable document.

\- Excellent: Extremely well structured, written, and presented document.



\## Evaluation plan in place (LO5, LO6, LO3)

\- Inadequate: No evaluation plan.

\- Adequate: Evaluation plan is very general and lacks metrics or methodology.

\- Good: Clear evaluation plan with metrics tied to objectives and appropriate methods.

\- Very Good: Detailed and rigorous evaluation plan with explicit success criteria and strong analytical methods.

\- Excellent: Exemplary, professional, and multifaceted evaluation plan covering technical, user, and ethical validation.



\## Proof of concept complete and/or good evidence of technical progress (LO3)

\- Inadequate: No working code or trivial unrelated code.

\- Adequate: Minimal technical progress with no convincing proof of concept.

\- Good: Clear evidence of technical progress and a basic proof of concept.

\- Very Good: Significant and measurable technical progress with a robust proof of concept.

\- Excellent: Exceptional technical progress demonstrating advanced practical skills and strongly de-risking the final deliverable.



\## Evidence of participation in supervision and responsiveness to feedback (LO1, LO6)

\- Inadequate: No evidence of attendance or response to feedback.

\- Adequate: Basic attendance, but feedback is not clearly documented or acted upon.

\- Good: Good evidence of engagement with supervision, with feedback documented and incorporated.

\- Very Good: Proactive engagement with supervision leading to substantive improvements.

\- Excellent: Exemplary professional engagement and strategic use of supervision.



\## Viable project plan regularly updated using an appropriate management tool (LO1, LO6)

\- Inadequate: Plan is not updated or no tracking tool is used.

\- Adequate: Tracking tool used superficially or irregularly.

\- Good: Plan is updated regularly using a suitable management tool and shows logical progress.

\- Very Good: Tool is used effectively to track tasks, dependencies, and risk.

\- Excellent: Mastery of project management techniques with meticulous maintenance and expert foresight.

\# Report



\## Correct referencing (LO7, LO2)

\- Inadequate: No references or inconsistent style.

\- Adequate: Sources are listed, but referencing style has frequent errors.

\- Good: Correct and consistent application of the chosen referencing style throughout.

\- Very Good: Referencing is flawless and highly professional.

\- Excellent: Referencing is impeccable and strategically used to support arguments.



\## Clear written expression (LO7)

\- Inadequate: Report is confusing and contains major grammar/spelling problems.

\- Adequate: Report is understandable but contains awkward phrasing and inconsistent tone.

\- Good: Clear, concise, and professional writing with logical structure.

\- Very Good: Highly articulate and persuasive writing.

\- Excellent: Exceptional technical communication with precision and elegance.



\## A thorough account of the technical implementation reflecting the project stages and key challenges (LO7)

\- Inadequate: Implementation is vague and major stages or decisions are omitted.

\- Adequate: Implementation is described superficially and lacks technical depth.

\- Good: Thorough and logical account of implementation, technical decisions, challenges, and solutions.

\- Very Good: Detailed and analytical account linking implementation back to design decisions, with strong evidence.

\- Excellent: Forensic-quality implementation narrative integrating diagrams and code examples.



\## A thorough and balanced evaluation of the project deliverable against the original aims and objectives, backed by evidence (LO5, LO3)

\- Inadequate: No evaluation or purely subjective evaluation.

\- Adequate: Basic statement of success without strong evidence or balance.

\- Good: Thorough and balanced evaluation using specific evidence and analytical skills.

\- Very Good: Highly objective and critical evaluation discussing both successes and failures.

\- Excellent: Exemplary evaluation generating novel insights into quality, usability, and future potential.



\## Viable and evidence-based plan for further work (LO3, LO5)

\- Inadequate: No future work plan.

\- Adequate: Future work suggestions are vague or weakly justified.

\- Good: Viable and evidence-based future work plan clearly derived from evaluation.

\- Very Good: Strategically insightful future work plan proposing significant next steps.

\- Excellent: Highly innovative and transformative roadmap with impeccable justification.



\## Insightful reflection showing the personal learning journey of the student (LO1, LO5, LO7)

\- Inadequate: No meaningful reflection.

\- Adequate: Reflection is superficial.

\- Good: Insightful reflection on challenges, successes, learning, and self-management.

\- Very Good: Highly critical and deeply honest reflection linked to self-management and critical self-evaluation.

\- Excellent: Outstanding reflection demonstrating maturity, professional development, and documented self-correction.



\## Evidence of ongoing and constructive engagement with supervisor and peer group (meeting notes) (LO1, LO6)

\- Inadequate: No meaningful meeting evidence.

\- Adequate: Minimal evidence and reactive engagement.

\- Good: Clear and complete meeting notes showing constructive engagement.

\- Very Good: Detailed, organised, and professional notes showing proactive incorporation of advice.

\- Excellent: Exemplary notes showing exceptional professional responsibility and self-management.

\# Artefact



\## Evidence-based solution design (LO2, LO3, LO4)

\- Inadequate: Design is arbitrary or copied.

\- Adequate: Solution design is functional, but justification from evidence is weak.

\- Good: Solution design is clearly informed by evidence and key decisions show analytical skill.

\- Very Good: Design is expertly and meticulously justified by evidence, resulting in a highly effective solution.

\- Excellent: Design is innovative and optimal, with every component traceable to evidence.



\## Use of industry standard design and documentation methods (LO2, LO3, LO4)

\- Inadequate: No usable design diagrams or documentation.

\- Adequate: Basic diagrams exist but are incomplete, inconsistent, or unprofessional.

\- Good: Appropriate use of industry-standard methods such as UML, ERD, or system flowcharts.

\- Very Good: Documentation is comprehensive, accurate, and professional, helping understanding and maintenance.

\- Excellent: Documentation is exemplary and significantly enhances clarity and maintainability.



\## Well planned and executed project development lifecycle (LO2, LO3, LO4, LO5)

\- Inadequate: No evidence of project stages or evaluation; development was reactive.

\- Adequate: Basic stages are identifiable, but lifecycle and evaluation are weak.

\- Good: Development follows a clear, structured lifecycle with iterative improvement.

\- Very Good: Lifecycle is proactively executed and documented with strong feedback loops and course correction.

\- Excellent: Demonstrates mastery of lifecycle management, resulting in a high-quality professional deliverable.



\## A well-judged project scope, appropriate to the aims and objectives of the project and skills of the student (LO2, LO4, LO5, LO1)

\- Inadequate: Project is too simple or wildly unachievable.

\- Adequate: Scope is generally achievable but not well judged.

\- Good: Scope is realistic, challenging, and delivers a complete solution.

\- Very Good: Scope is optimal, highly challenging, innovative, and manageable.

\- Excellent: Scope represents exemplary challenge and professional-level judgement.



\## Appropriate and well justified application of computer science / software engineering knowledge and skills to the problem (LO2, LO4, LO3, LO5)

\- Inadequate: Skills applied are trivial or unsuitable.

\- Adequate: Application of skills is mostly descriptive and weakly justified.

\- Good: Appropriate and justified application of core technical skills to solve the defined real need.

\- Very Good: Expert application of specialist knowledge with rigorous justification of technical decisions.

\- Excellent: Outstanding mastery and innovation in applying advanced knowledge and skills.



\## Clear evidence of development of the student's knowledge and skills increasing through the project stages (LO3, LO5, LO1)

\- Inadequate: No evidence of new learning.

\- Adequate: Some new skills were learned, but application is weak.

\- Good: Clear evidence of new skills acquired and applied across the project.

\- Very Good: Significant and measurable growth in skills is evident.

\- Excellent: Exceptional knowledge development and proactive self-directed learning.



\## Technical skills applied with attention to industry standard best practices (LO3, LO1, LO5)

\- Inadequate: Code is spaghetti-like, undocumented, and lacks best practices.

\- Adequate: Functional code but poorly formatted, weakly documented, and minimal architecture.

\- Good: Good adherence to clean code, formatting, version control, and internal documentation.

\- Very Good: Practices are consistently professional with strong maintainability, testing, and quality attention.

\- Excellent: Exemplary best practices with secure, maintainable, rigorously tested architecture.



\## Legal, ethical and professional issues identified are evident in the technical implementation (LO6, LO2)

\- Inadequate: Legal/ethical/professional issues are ignored in the code.

\- Adequate: Minimal acknowledgment with weak mitigation.

\- Good: Technical implementation demonstrably addresses key identified issues.

\- Very Good: Mitigations are built into the core design using professional standards.

\- Excellent: Artefact is a model of ethical design with advanced mitigations.



\## Testing and evaluation conducted using industry standard practices (LO5, LO3, LO6)

\- Inadequate: No meaningful testing conducted.

\- Adequate: Basic testing is done but lacks documentation or strong metrics.

\- Good: Appropriate testing is documented and evaluation uses clear evidence-based metrics.

\- Very Good: Rigorous and systematic testing is demonstrated, including strong objective evaluation.

\- Excellent: Testing is exemplary and demonstrates mastery of testing methodologies.



\## A critical evaluation of the contribution of the solution to the original problem identified (LO5, LO2, LO1)

\- Inadequate: No meaningful assessment of effectiveness or contribution.

\- Adequate: General statement of success without real measurement or analysis.

\- Good: Solution contribution is clearly evaluated against the original problem using evidence.

\- Very Good: Highly critical and objective assessment of real-world contribution, including shortcomings.

\- Excellent: Outstanding evidence-based final assessment offering novel insights and exceptional critical thinking.
